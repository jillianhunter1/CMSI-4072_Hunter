*“The Miracle” by Noni Olabisi*

Is this piece ART or CRAFT? Is it perhaps BOTH?
- This piece is art, primarily because its value lies in its conceptual and emotional weight rather than its utility. However, it displays a high level of craft in its execution, specifically the anatomical shading of the hands and feet and the deliberate use of the yellow halo. In many ways, it is both. Craft is the "how", while art is the "why".
What about this selection drew you to it or caught your eye?
- What immediately catches my eye is the chromatic contrast. The deep, dark blues of the infant’s skin set against the fiery reds of the background create an instant pop.
What kinds of feelings does the piece elicit when you see it?
- The large, looming hands suggest a cosmic or ancestral presence. There is a feeling of "the calm within the storm", a new life entering a chaotic world but being held by something greater. It evokes a sense of vulnerability and divine protection.
What do you think the artist is trying to say with the piece?
- Given the imagery, the artist likely explores themes of creation, the halo suggests a "Holy Infant," perhaps reimagining traditional divinity. It speaks to the sacredness of life amidst struggle.
What does the piece say to YOU personally regardless of what the artist is saying?
- I see a representation of emergence. The child represents a new consciousness appearing out of the chaotic lightning and red clouds. It reminds me that even complex systems start from a singular, fragile point of origin.
Do you think Artificial Intelligence could produce something of equal meaning?
- No. Digital colors do not pop the same as oil on canvas (or whatever mediums). An artist puts their lived experience - their pain, their heritage, their physical heartbeat- into the brush. AI lacks "lived experience." While it can synthesize meaning for a viewer, it don't "feel" the holiness of the halo while its generating pixels. The meaning often resides more in the human who views it than in the machine that made it.
How do you think you might apply the concept of art or craft to your software designs and coding?
- The craft  is the actual code aspect. It’s the syntax, the efficient algorithms, and the  architecture. It’s making sure the "plumbing" of the app doesn’t leak. The art is the User Experience and the creative problem solving.
Do you think software creation SHOULD BE an art? Should it be a craft?
 - Software that is only craft is functional but soulless; it’s a tool that works but no one loves. Software that is only art is beautiful but broken; it’s an idea that crashes.
What kinds of questions do you ask yourself about your design/code? Do you go back to it after it is working and re-factor it to make it clean?
-  When looking at code, I ask myself 1) is it readable? and 2) is it nice?. By readability, I want my code to be able to be reviewed by a software engineer and have them be able to easily understand it and follow it. I also want my code to be nice. I want the layout to be non-confusing, I want the UX to look seamless. I absolutely go back and refactor my code. I always go in and delete "useless" comments I make for myself, I also make sure the spacing is consistent. Sometimes, I may go back and opitimize the code if the processign time is super slow or laggy.
